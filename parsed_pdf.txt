The current 3D visualization has two critical issues:
- No depth - When viewed from the side, all nodes collapse into a near-vertical line. The Z-axis is barely used. This is
a 3D model but looks 2D.

The goal is to match the aesthetic of a neural network brain visualization where colored node clusters form recognizable

What Needs to Change
1. Replace REGION_GRAVITY with Anatomically Correct 3D Brain Coordinates
The current positions are wrong - they don't form a brain shape. Replace them with coordinates that place each region
where it actually sits in a real brain, using a right-lateral view as the primary orientation (brain facing right). The
coordinate system is: X = left/right (medial/lateral), Y = up/down (superior/inferior), Z = front/back (anterior/posterior).
Think about it anatomically:
- Frontal Lobe - The largest region. Front-top of the brain. Fills the entire anterior portion, from the top curving down to
above the eyes. Should be a large, wide volume in the front-upper area.
- Parietal Lobe - Top-center/back of the brain, sits behind the frontal lobe on the crown. Upper-middle area.
- Temporal Lobe - Sides of the brain, below the parietal and behind the frontal. Sits at ear-level, extending along the
lateral sides. Lower-middle area.
- Occipital Lobe - The very back of the brain. Rear portion, slightly lower than the parietal.
- Cerebellum - Below and behind the occipital lobe. The "little brain" at the base-back. Lower-rear, distinct ball-like
region.
- Brain Stem - The lowest point, extending downward from the center-bottom. Connects to the spinal cord.
Center-bottom, slightly forward of the cerebellum.
- Limbic System - Deep interior, center of the brain. Hidden inside, between the temporal lobes.
- Amygdala - Deep interior, slightly forward and below the limbic system. Near the medial temporal lobe area.
- Corpus Callosum - The bridge connecting left and right hemispheres. Runs as a flat band through the center, above
the limbic system.

2. Distribute Nodes in 3D Volumes, Not on Planes
The current code seeds nodes like this:
x: gravity.x + (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20,
y: gravity.y + (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20,
z: gravity.z + (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20,
This creates a tiny 20-unit cube around each centroid. Instead, each region should define its own bounding volume (an
ellipsoid or box) that matches the anatomical shape of that lobe. Nodes should be seeded randomly within that volume
using a spherical/ellipsoidal distribution (not a cube - brains are rounded). The spread should be:
- Large lobes (frontal, parietal, temporal): ~40-50 unit radius ellipsoids
- Medium regions (occipital, cerebellum): ~25-35 unit radius
- Small/deep regions (limbic, amygdala, brain_stem, corpus_callosum): ~15-25 unit radius
Use a proper 3D point-in-ellipsoid distribution. Example approach:
// Spherical random point within ellipsoid (rx, ry, rz)
const theta = Math.random() * Math.PI * 2;
const phi = Math.acos(2 * Math.random() - 1);
const r = Math.cbrt(Math.random()); // cube root for uniform volume
const x = center.x + rx * r * Math.sin(phi) * Math.cos(theta);
const y = center.y + ry * r * Math.sin(phi) * Math.sin(theta);
const z = center.z + rz * r * Math.cos(phi);
3. Increase Region Gravity Force
The current gravity multiplier is 0.06, which is too weak - nodes drift away from their region. Increase it to 0.12-0.15 so
nodes stay within their lobe's volume while still having organic movement. Also increase the initial seed spread
accordingly.
4. Tune Force Simulation for Brain Shape Preservation
- Charge (repulsion): Reduce from -60 to about -30 or -40. Strong repulsion blows the brain shape apart.
- Link distance: Reduce from 25 to 15. Nodes within the same lobe should stay closer together.
- Center force: Reduce from 0.05 to 0.02 or remove entirely. Center force pulls everything into a ball, destroying the
brain silhouette. Region gravity should be the dominant positioning force.
- Velocity decay: Increase from 0.4 to 0.5 or 0.6 for faster settling.
5. Camera Position
Update the default camera to frame the brain nicely from a 3/4 perspective:
camera={{ position: [80, 40, 120], fov: 55 }}
